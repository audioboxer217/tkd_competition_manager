# TKD Competition Manager — Volunteer Guide

For general support staff assisting with athlete flow, check-in, and ring-side support.

---

## 1) Your role

As a volunteer your primary responsibilities are:

- Helping athletes find their ring and division
- Supporting the check-in and staging process
- Relaying no-shows, late arrivals, and issues to ring staff or admin
- **Not** entering match results or making data changes (unless specifically assigned and trained)

---

## 2) Pages you may use

| Page | URL | Purpose |
|------|-----|---------|
| Live View | `/` | Read-only public view of current ring status |
| Brackets and Tournament Results | `/results` | Read-only public results view |

If you need to know the current state of a ring or division, first check the Live View. 
Then ask the ring operator or administrator if unsure.

---

## 3) Day-of support tasks

### Check-in support

1. Verify athlete names against the official registration list.
2. Confirm the athlete is in the correct division and ring.
3. Direct athletes to their staging area.
4. Do **not** make changes to competitor records — if something looks wrong, report it to admin.

### Staging and call-up

1. Call up athletes in the order given by the ring operator.
2. Notify ring staff immediately if an athlete is missing or late.
3. For any no-show, give the ring operator enough time to escalate before a match is started.

### Relay and communication

- Keep a clear, direct communication line with your assigned ring operator.
- Report issues promptly — do not wait to see if they resolve themselves.

---

## 4) Escalation examples

Report these immediately to the on-duty administrator:

| Issue | Who to tell |
|-------|------------|
| Athlete's name does not match any roster entry | Admin |
| Possible duplicate athlete record | Admin |
| Athlete assigned to the wrong ring or division | Admin |
| Competitor name on screen does not match the athlete present | Admin |
| Ring operator cannot save a result | Admin |

**Suggested escalation format:**

> "Ring [X] / Division [Y] / Match [Z]: [brief description].  
> Observed on screen: [what you saw].  
> Needs admin review."

---

## 5) Do and don't

### Do
- Verify names carefully before directing athletes
- Communicate clearly and quickly with ring staff
- Escalate uncertain data problems without delay

### Don't
- Modify bracket structure or division assignments
- Change ring sequence numbers
- Submit unverified or guessed data corrections
- Start or stop a match — that is the ring operator's responsibility

---

## 6) End of shift

- Let your supervisor know you are leaving.
- Hand off any open issues to the on-duty administrator before departing.
- Do not leave any athletes in staging without informing ring staff.
